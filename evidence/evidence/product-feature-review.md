# Product Feature Review — BalanceAI Session 14

## 1. Hypothesis
Students trust and use an AI-generated weekly plan more than their current
manual planning method.

## 2. MVP Experiment
Show one real student the BalanceAI prototype for 10-15 minutes. Let them
add their own real tasks, do the stress/energy check-in, and generate a
weekly plan. Observe how they use the accept, edit, and dismiss controls.

## 3. Target User
A busy university student aged 18-25, living away from home, with at least
3 active deadlines this week and no consistent planning method.

## 4. Feedback Questions
- "Show me how you would add your tasks for this week."
- "What would you do with this recommendation — and why?"
- "Does this plan feel realistic for your actual week?"
- "What is missing or confusing?"

## 5. Key Learning
To be filled in after running the experiment.
Example: "The student accepted 2 recommendations but dismissed the recovery
block because it felt forced."

## 6. Next Changes
To be filled in after running the experiment.
Example: "Make recovery blocks optional and explain why they are suggested."
